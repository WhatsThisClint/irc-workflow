# Study Design Construction

Produce a concrete, field-ready design tied directly to the conceptual model — every design choice should trace back to a variable or relationship named there.

## For institutional/social-science questions (e.g. WUC comparisons)
1. **Sampling frame and size**: state how many units (WUCs, villages, households) and why — reasoning should reference the comparison being made (e.g. "paired sample of N directly-funded and N cess-dependent WUCs, matched on command area size and years since formation, to isolate the funding-modality effect from scale/age confounds"). Give an actual number with brief reasoning, not just "a sample of WUCs."
2. **Identification/recruitment method**: how the specific units will be identified (e.g. from ACIWRM's WUC registry, stratified by funding modality and district) — not "convenience sample."
3. **Respondent list**: who is interviewed and why, tied explicitly to the model's variables (e.g. WUC president/secretary for governance/fund-utilization variables, a sample of member farmers for perceived-legitimacy and compliance variables, an independent infrastructure assessment for the management-quality DV rather than relying solely on self-report).
4. **Infrastructure/outcome assessment method**: if the DV involves a physical/observable outcome (canal condition, maintenance quality), specify how it will be independently assessed (site visit checklist, not just interview claims) — self-reported management quality is a common validity gap to avoid baking in.
5. **Confounder handling**: name the controls from the conceptual model and how each will be measured or matched on.

## For biophysical/remote-sensing questions (e.g. NDVI source attribution)
1. **Data sources**: satellite product(s) and resolution, time period/crop cycle coverage, and why that product fits the question's spatial/temporal scale.
2. **Comparison design**: how canal-irrigated vs. well-irrigated (or equivalent comparison) plots will be identified on the ground for the comparison — this usually still needs some ground-truthing/field verification, not remote data alone.
3. **Validation approach**: how the remote-sensing signal will be checked against ground truth (farmer-reported or field-verified irrigation source for a subsample) — a report with no validation step is the single most common gap `inception-report-review` would flag here.
4. **Confounders**: crop type, sowing date, soil type — state how each will be controlled for or stratified.

## General
- State study duration implications directly into Step 5 (work plan) — don't leave sampling/fieldwork timing vague.
- If the question is genuinely mixed (institutional + biophysical), design both tracks and explicitly state how/where their outputs will be integrated (e.g. infrastructure assessment score feeding into the institutional management-quality DV).
