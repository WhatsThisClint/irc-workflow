# ELI5 + 80/20 Framing — Construction Method

Mirrors `inception-report-review`'s closing method, but framed forward: instead of naming the top *gap* in an existing report, name the top *design choice the study's credibility rests on*.

## 1. First-principles restatement
In 1-2 plain sentences, strip all jargon and state what the study is actually trying to find out — the version a non-technical stakeholder would recognize immediately. E.g. "This study is trying to find out whether farmers pay a canal fee because of how the fee system is designed, not just because some farmers are more willing to pay in general."

## 2. The 80/20 design choice
Across the conceptual model, literature framing, and study design just built, identify the single choice most of the study's credibility rests on — usually one of:
- The comparison/matching logic in the sampling design (does it actually isolate the IV's effect from confounds?), or
- The validation step for a key measurement (does the DV actually measure what it claims to?), or
- A specific mediator/moderator being modeled at all (does the design capture the real mechanism, or just a correlation?).

State it explicitly: "If this study gets [X] right, everything else is secondary; if it gets [X] wrong, the rest of the design can't rescue the conclusion." This is deliberately the same test `inception-report-review` would apply later — naming it here means the researcher already knows where the scrutiny will land.

## Output form
2-4 short sentences total: one first-principles restatement, one 80/20 call-out with a one-line reason.
