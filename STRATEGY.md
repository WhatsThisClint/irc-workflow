# India Research Corps Strategy

## Target problem

The Coordinator cannot track student research quality, mentor responsiveness, and cohort bottlenecks at scale, making it hard to verify scientific rigor and prove competency growth to secure funding.

## Our approach

Integrate a local n8n workflow system with a local multi-agent AI system (Gemini 3.5 Flash) to automate onboarding, daily progress checks (Academic Triad & Sponsor Alignment), and Friday digest compilation, allowing the Coordinator to manage the entire cohort lifecycle asynchronously.

## Who it's for

**Primary:** The Academic and Operations Coordinator at WELL Labs, Bangalore - They're hiring India Research Corps to automate administrative tasks, audit research quality, flag bottlenecks, and generate cohort reports automatically.

## Key metrics

- **Academic Triad growth slope** - Tracking changes in methodology, triangulation, and clarity scores over time.
- **Sponsor Alignment Index** - Measuring research relevance to sponsors' water problems.
- **Cohort Success Velocity** - Average delay days categorized by cohort phase vs. historical baseline.

## Tracks

### Onboarding Automation
Automating folder creation, expert baseline generation, and Asana board structuring.
_Why it serves the approach:_ Simplifies administrative startup tasks so the coordinator can focus on qualitative onboarding check-ins.

### Daily Monitor & Auditing
Continuous tracking of Gantt charts, Academic Triad & Sponsor Alignment checks, mentor comments, and automatic nudges.
_Why it serves the approach:_ Provides real-time quality and timeline visibility without the need for manual check-ins.

### Cohort Dashboard & Reporting
Airtable metrics logging and weekly Friday Director Digest email generation.
_Why it serves the approach:_ Aggregates active red flags and competency trajectory data for leadership and funding presentations.

## Marketing

**One-liner:** Empirical environmental research backed by academic excellence and direct partner alignment.

**Key message:** The India Research Corps guides students through hands-on, rigorous socio-hydrology research, matching them with local agencies to solve real-world water crises with academic integrity.
